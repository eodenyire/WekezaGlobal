## 2ï¸âƒ£ Key Problems

### A. Fragmented Banking Ecosystem

* Many African countries have **dozens of banks**, each with different APIs, settlement rules, and processing times.

* Current systems do **not interoperate seamlessly**, increasing operational complexity.

### B. Slow and Costly Cross-Border Payments

* International platforms like PayPal or Wise charge **high fees and FX spreads**.
* Settlements can take **days**, particularly when local banks are intermediaries.
* Currency conversion is often **suboptimal**, eroding earnings.

**Impact:** Loss of income, reduced competitiveness, and low adoption of global digital services.

---

### C. Limited Access to Global Financial Infrastructure

* Most African freelancers, exporters, and SMEs **cannot get USD/EUR/GBP collection accounts easily**.
* Card issuing for foreign currency is often **restricted or expensive**.
* Access to credit or liquidity against foreign earnings is largely unavailable.

**Impact:** Businesses are cash-constrained, and individuals cannot leverage earnings for growth or consumption.

---

### D. Lack of Intelligent Settlement and FX Optimization

* Earnings often sit idle in local bank accounts before being converted or used.
* No platform currently **optimizes liquidity routing** across multiple banks for speed or cost.

**Impact:** Suboptimal FX, delayed access to funds, and revenue leakage.

---

### E. Limited Infrastructure for Fintech Integration

* African fintechs often rely on **single banks** or third-party providers.
* This creates a **single point of failure** and slows scaling.

**Impact:** Tech startups struggle to access global payment rails efficiently.
